The Interaction Model

1. Context Over Prompts.
Your cofounder isn't a blank slate. It maintains ongoing context about your product, audience, and goals. When you need something done, you don't write a complex prompt—you provide a brief, just like you would to a human partner.

2. Asynchronous Execution.
You give direction. Your cofounder goes to work. Depending on the complexity of the task, it works asynchronously, allowing you to step away and focus on product building while it handles growth, research, or operations.

3. Deliverables, Not Suggestions.
When you brief your cofounder on a go-to-market strategy, it doesn't give you a list of ideas. It produces a finished document. Output is execution-grade and ready to use.

Example Output

Here is an actual output from cofounder.new. This is the standard of work.

OUTPUT • 4 MINUTES

Go-To-Market Strategy: Q3 Launch

TARGET ARCHETYPE: Solo Technical Founder

BRIEF: "We are launching the new developer API next week. I need a distribution plan focusing on indie hackers and small dev teams."

1. Positioning & Core Narrative

The core message is speed to integration. We are not selling "robust enterprise infrastructure"—we are selling "get this working in a weekend."

2. Distribution Channels

  • Product Hunt: Launch scheduled for Tuesday. Maker comment drafted.
  • Twitter/X: 5-part thread drafted focusing on the technical problem we solved while building this API.
  • Hacker News: "Show HN" draft prepared. Focus on the raw latency benchmarks.

3. Immediate Action Items

1. Review the attached Product Hunt maker comment.

2. Approve the Twitter thread.

3. Add the tracking snippet to the new API docs page.

Not a prompt library.

It's a cofounder. The distinction is the point.
